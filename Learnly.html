Learnly

Overview

An APP for university students to solve academic problems through learning communities

Learnly was created in the Xchange Program, aiming to address the issue of low engagement of university students in learning communities for solving academic problems in Taiwan. Our goal is to boost students' motivation to ask questions and participate in learning communities. Learnly encourages users to support each other, validate shared challenges, and access answers easily.

Role

UX research and design

Team

Designers * 3

Tool

Figma

Timeline

2022 Jan. - 2023 July

Process

Schedule the project following the double-diamond model

Background

University students in Taiwan show low involvement in learning communities

A learning community is a space where university students gather to discuss class assignments and exchange information. Studies have shown that participating in learning communities can enhance students' motivation to learned. However, we've observed that in Taiwan, university students are less willing to engage in these communities, even when their classes provide online forums or other social groups. Therefore, our goal is to enhance the engagement of learning communities among university students, hoping that it would help students better understand the course material and solve academic problems more efficiently.

Survey

Only half of university students engage in learning community discussions

We conducted a questionnaire survey to validate our observations and found that university students frequently visit learning communities but rarely participate in discussions.

Research

Uncover users' motivation, pain points, and benefits in learning communities

Through interviews, we were able to gain insights into users' experiences, behaviors, and underlying pain points in learning communities. Our goals were to:

  • Understand motivations and patterns of university students in learning communities

  • Understand experience and feelings of university students in learning communities

  • Identify areas for improvement of learning commmunivties

Synthesize Interview Results

Step 1：Categorize data from 8 interviews based on learning community platforms

Step 2：Group by similar insights based on motivations, pain points, and benefits of using different learning platforms

Key Insights

University students with academic questions
need to find a supportive learning environment
because it's important for them to learn without fear of judgment.

Ideation

Brainstorm design solutions through HMW

Divide the process into 3 stages and select ideas align with the PoV

Wireflow

Clarify the operation of the system

We crafted wireframes to ensure consensus on users' interaction with the system, provide direction for design, and facilitate testing and iterations.

Testing and Iteration

Make 3 iterations based on user feedback

We tested the design with 8 users to understand whether they:

1. The reward mechanism is unclear and lacks sufficient incentives

After successfully posting a question and receiving a scoring reward, users expressed confusion about the points earned for asking questions. They mentioned that their primary concern was whether the problem was resolved, rather than the quantity of rewards earned.

Improvement

Remove the reward mechanism for giving points and keep the animation after posting a question.

2. The information on the main page is unclear

While browsing the homepage, users suggested that topics with more viewers should be displayed first. Moreover, some users expressed confusion regarding the meaning of the heart icon on the screen.

Improvement

Include viewer and like counts on each post, and sort the posts based on the number of viewers.

  • Viewer Count: the number of people viewing the question

  • Like Count: the number of people like the question

3. The way the information presented in the chat room can be improved

Users reported that displaying content in dialogue boxes can lead to messages easily being covered by other messages, so they have to scroll through conversations to find answers each time.

Improvment

  • Posters can set a messege as the best solution when they think the question is solved.

  • The "Best Solution" will always be on the top of the chat room.

After user testing, we found that university students in learning communities
not only value a supportive learning environment
but also need easy access to answers, so that they can efficiently achieve their learning goals.

Solution

3 key features solving pain points and enhance benefits

Solving Pain Points

Create a friendly and supportive community environment

  • Clapping animation encourages users to express agreement, fostering a positive community environment

  • Users also gain confidence in asking or answering questions when they receive applause from others.

Solving Pain Points

Enable users to quickly confirm whether others have similar problems

  • Automatically suggest relevant posts when entering a question to help users find answers more efficiently

  • Knowing that others have similar questions alleviates users' concerns about judgment from others

Enhance benefits

Enhance the convenience for users to access answers

  • The "Best Answer" feature saves users' time on browsing chat rooms and searching for solutions

Reflection
1. The importance of testing the design with users

After user testing, we found that users in learning communities care not just about the sence of belonging but also value getting answers quickly. This unexpected finding uncovered the importance of validating assumptions made during research to make better design decisions that better meet user needs.

2. Future work

The learning process requires consistent observation. Hence, we aim to continuously refine the workflow of each feature, catering to various learning scenarios. We will consistently conduct testing and iterate to develop solutions that closely align with user needs.
