Overview

An APP for university students to solve academic problems through online community

A learning community is a space where university students come together to discuss class assignments and share information. Research has shown that engaging in a learning community can enhance people's motivation to learn. However, we noticed that in Taiwan, many university students are not willing to create or engage in such communities, even when their classes provide their own discussion forums or other social groups. Therefore, we aimed to enhance the engagement of the learning community, hoping to facilitate students' comprehension of the course content.

Problem

Lack of information on each page and unclear system prompts cause confusion for students and teaching assistants

While using PDOGs, students often face difficulties due to the lack of sufficient information on each page. This leads to lengthy processes when checking information or submitting assignments. Similarly, teaching assistants find the operations somewhat complex, and the system prompts are not clear enough. In the past, they had to rely on oral communication to instruct the next semester's teaching assistants on how to operate the system. As a result, we have decided to redesign the pages in order to address the issues faced by both students and teaching assistants.

Research

Uncover 4 problems through heuristic evaluation and interviews

1. Heuristic Evaluation for Students

As all members in the Design Team have prior experience using PDOGs, we conducted a heuristic evaluation to understand students' experiences. We found that the main challenge of the current platform was the scattered presentation of information, leading to a lengthy process for retrieving information and submitting assignments.

2. Interview Teaching Assistants

We conducted interviews with 6 TAs from the two Information Management courses with the highest student enrollment. The interview process was conducted by asking participants to showcase their typical operations on the PDOGs website, then pointing out areas where they have encountered inconvenience or confusion. Our goals were to:

  • Understand the behaviors, experiences and feelings of TAs using PDOGs

  • Understand the challenges encountered by TAs while using PDOGs

  • Identify areas for improvements of PDOGs

Reflection
1. Improve the ability of time management and team collaboration

In my first experience as a design leader, I was responsible for defining project goals and managing the schedule from the beginning. This was an invaluable learning experience for me, as I realized the value of ensuring that every team member comprehends their responsibilities well and incorporating the requirements and timelines of the development team into the decision-making process.

2. The challenge of considering two users in design

This project involves both student and teaching assistant roles. While these roles are not entirely conflicting, it's easy to overlook details related to one side during the design process. There's also a need to balance the importance of different features for each role.

3. Constraints when improving existing system

During the initial design phase, we came up with several ideas which were later discarded after discussing with the development team. I learned that when improving an established system, it's important to avoid getting carried away with fanciful ideas. Instead, we should focus on finding feasible enhancements within the existing framework.
