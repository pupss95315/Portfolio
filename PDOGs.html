Overview

PDOGs is an online judging system designed for students enrolled in courses provided by the Department of Information Management at National Taiwan University. Students can use the platform to submit coding assignments, which are then graded by TAs via the system. PDOGs had a huge update in 2020, which improved the entire experience. However, recognizing opportunities for further improvement, we refined the presentation of information and provided clearer prompts, which made it easier for students and TAs to complete tasks.

Project Timeline

Discover the Problem

Research 1: Heuristic Evaluation for Students

As all members of the Design Team were prior PDOGs users, we conducted a heuristic evaluation to uncover potential areas for improvements. We found that the main challenge of the current platform was the scattered presentation of information, resulting in a time-consuming process for retrieving information and submitting assignments.

Research 2: Interviews with TAs

Participants: 6 TAs

Research Goals:

  • Understand the behaviors, experiences and feelings of TAs using PDOGs

  • Understand the challenges encountered by TAs while using PDOGs

  • Identify areas for improvements of PDOGs

2. Redesign sidebar

The return button in the previous system's sidebar can easily cause confusion, as it navigated to the prior layer of the sysem rather than the previous page. As a result, we added the name of the previous layer to notify users where they are shifting. Aside from that, we added icons showing the assignment's status to encourage users to submit their assignments.

4. Clearer Cues for TA

In the TA system, there are several predefined phrases and format for data input. However, the system lacks explicit prompts to guide TAs on the meaning of specific terms and system operation. Hence, we solved these issues by offering more explanations, and clearer placeholder and error messages.

Reflection

01Enhance time management and team collaboration

It was an invaluable learning experience for me as my first time being a leader of the design team. I learned that it's crucial to ensure team members are aware of their responsibilities and that the development team's requirements and timelines are taken into account during the decision-making process.

02 Design for two types of users

This project involved both student and teaching assistant roles. While these roles are not entirely conflicting, it's easy to overlook details related to one side during the design process. There's also a need to balance the importance of different features for each role.

03 Overcome constraints of established system

In the beginning of the design phase, we came up with multiple ideas, but we discarded most of them after discussing with the development team. I realized that when improving an existing system, it's crucial to avoid getting carried away with imaginative ideas. Instead, we should focus on identifying practical improvements that can be made within the existing framework.
