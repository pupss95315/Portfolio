Overview

A website designed to improve information sharing among exchange students

Swing NTU is a social platform designed and developed in the Web Programming Course with an aim of improving the experience of information sharing among exchange students using online shared spreadsheets. The platform empowers exchange students with intuitive sorting, keyword searches, and robust data protection, fostering efficient and reliable communication. Swing NTU creates a secure and convenient environment for exchange students to find and share information throughout the application process.

Problem

Exchange students struggle to seek information using the shared spread sheet

When I was applying for the exchange program, I found myself in a state of confusion when it came to filling out the application forms. That's when I saw a shared spreadsheet in the school forum, where students who also applied for exchange programs shared information and the schools they intended to apply to. Based on the content in the spreadsheet, I found it more clear which schools I might choose from. However, I soon realized that the spreadsheet's usability was lacking. There were two problems regarding the shared spead sheet.

1. Disorganized interface resulted in a real challenge to find specific details

The comments failed to follow a specific direction or order. In order to emphasize the importance of their comments, users highlighted them in different colors, leading to the chaotic interface. Therefore, as they navigate through the content, they must rely on "arrows" to find the context of the comments.

2. Unable to distinguish the functions of the application form and the comment section.

Beside the application form, applicants from the same group would directly discuss group-related information, leading to a cluttered interface and making it difficult to distinguish which information was related to application preferences.

Research

Existing comments revealed 2 user goals and 3 pain points using the shared spread sheet

I conducted secondary research by observing users' comments in past spreadsheets to gain a deeper understanding of user characteristics and validating my observation of potential issues with the spreadsheet usage. By summarizing the comments, I identified 2 key motivations to engage in the spreadsheet discussion and extracted 3 main pain points while using the shared spreadsheet.

Users’ Goals

  • Users want to clarify the information provided by the school with students who have experienced similar confusion

  • Users need information about application preferences of others to make decisions on filling the application form

For both types of users, all they need is an easy and efficient access to the desired information that can address their problems.

Users’ Pain Points
The low information credibility and confusing interface are the main reasons for a poor user experience!
Ideation

Information Architecture

We knew from the research that efficient information-seeking is the top priority of exchange students using the shared spreadsheet. After a thorough discussion, my teammate and I agreed that a forum-based social platform would be an ideal solution, since it provides comprehensive sorting and filtering mechanisms, allowing users to quickly find desired information. Therefore, we ideated potential features and outlined the platform structure.

Solution
Solution 1: Login

Users can verify their identity using their exchange student ID, gaining control over their own settings and preventing users from encountering unexpected changes.

Solution 2: Bulletin Board

1. Add the bulletin board shwoing group comments
Users can share group-related information within the group bulletin board, separating the comments that were originally next to the application form.

2. Keyword Search and Sorting Feature
Use different sorting criteria to prioritize important comments, allowing users to effectively find relevant information and navigate through copmments.

3. Save Comments
Users can save comments they find relevant or interesting, eliminating the need for them to search for the same content repeatedly.

Solution 3: Application Form

Users can view information for all members in the same group, and edit their own fields on the form.
