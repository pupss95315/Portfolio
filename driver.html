The Impact of In-car Navigation Systems on
Drivers' Moods

Overview

Explore in-car navigation systems' impact on drivers' feelings and behaviors

In the "Huma-computer Interaction and User Studies" course, my team and I conducted user research on the topic of "Drivers' Negative Emotions while Interacting with In-Car Navigation Systems". Through interviews with 12 participants who had experience using in-car navigation systems while driving, we found that in-car navigation systems have little impact on drivers' negative emotions during driving. However, the driving experience has shown to influence users' interactions with in-car navigation systems. The study not only enhanced our understanding of user behavior and experiences with in-car navigation systems but also provided insights for future design and improvements in in-car navigation systems.

Background

Does in-car navigation systems' drawbacks impact drivers' emotions?

Driving is one of the most prevalent methods of transportation for modern people. However, because of the continual threat of accidents, it requires a high level of concentration to stay focused. Drivers inevitably rely on navigation systems for guidance while driving. We've found that most in-car navigation systems are less convenient and complete than mobile applications, in terms of interface and functionality. This difference forces drivers to put in extra effort to become familiar with the interface of in-car navigation systems. As a result, in this study, we aim to understand whether the interaction between drivers and in-car navigation systems may result in negative emotions during the driving process.

Research
Interview

Participants：12 people who could drive and had experience using in-car navigation systems

Goal： understand drivers’ experience with the in-car navigation systems and their impacts on drivers' emotions

* Participants' ages varied significantly, so we divided them into three groups based on their driving experience: novice drivers with less than 10 years' experience, experienced drivers with 10-40 years' experience, and veteran drivers with more than 40 years' experience.

Affinity Diagram

We used 3 colors of sticky notes to represent the three groups of drivers, and grouped together similar responses. This visual approach enabled us to easily identified and uncovered underlying patterns within each group.

Results

Key Insight

1. The in-car navigation system has little impact on drivers' concentration and nervousness.

  • The in-car navigation systems usually serve as a supporting role and drivers barely interact with them while driving.

  • Inaccurate positioning and overly frequent vocal output may lead to increased nervousness.

  • Unclear vocal output may lead to distraction.

2. There are differences in how drivers with various experience interact with in-car navigation systems

  • Drivers with over 10 years' experience may have less experience with technology, so they tend to rely on their own judgement while finding the correct route.

  • Therefore, they are more likely to experience negative emotions while interacting with the in-car navigation systems.

3. In-car navigation systems are less easy-to-use compared to mobile phones

  • Users mentioned that slow touch or voice input are hard to use due to being used to mpre responsive devices such as mobile phones.

  • Future design can aim to reduce the gap in user adaptation to different interfaces.

Reflection
1. The conflict between user habits and new technologies

From experienced users' responses, it's evident that even with the emergence of more convenient technologies, they may stick to their familiar habits. Therefore, the new technologies become a challenge for them instead.

2. Limited number of respondents due to time constraints and data acquisition ease

It's worth noting that the gender distrbution is skewed, which may lead to a bias in research results toward male behaviors. Future research needs for larger-scale data collection to explore ideal usage patterns.
